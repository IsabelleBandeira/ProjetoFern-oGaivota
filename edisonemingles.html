  • Ignite perseverance with Thomas Edison

    Biography

    An inventor known for his many failures, long before he was known for his successes, Thomas Edison was considered "too dumb to learn anything", according to one of his first teachers. However, everyone knows the name of the man responsible for inventing the lamp - even though he made 1,001 attempts before he got it right. His perseverance with this invention clearly embodies his statement: "I have not failed 10,000 times - I have been successful because I have found 10,000 ways that do not work."

    He started early, with the support of his mother who taught him science and allowed him to practice in his own "mini-laboratory". At the age of 14, he suffered a train accident on the railroad he worked on, which gradually left him deaf. Some time later, he was fired, and found himself in financial difficulties. But they were all resolved when he sets up a telegraph that interested a president of a powerful company, who gave him $ 40,000 in exchange for the product. Finally, in 1879 and after carrying out about 1200 experiments, Thomas Edison invented the lamp and gave meaning to his famous phrase: "A genius is made up of 1% of inspiration and 99% of effort".
