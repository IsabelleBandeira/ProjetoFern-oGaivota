  • The philosophical story of J. K. Rowling

    Biography

    It all started well for J. K. Rowling, who grew up among many books, loved to write and make up stories from a very young age. Years later, she graduated in French from the University of Exeter and became a bilingual secretary, which was her parents' dream. However, she was fired for leaving aside some of the chores of the job to write her stories.

    It was during a train trip to London in 1990 that Rowling had her biggest idea: the little wizard Harry Potter. However, at the end of that year, her mother died and she, extremely sad, moved to the city of Porto in Portugal, where she started to teach English classes. And it was there that the writer finally started to organize and write ‘Harry Potter and the Philosopher’s Stone’. After being turned down 12 times, Bloomsbury finally agreed to publish the book, which did not take long to hit among children, teenagers and young people.
